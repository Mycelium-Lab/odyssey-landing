W2W vs W2A:
choosing the right model

By the Odyssey team·July 2026·7 min read

Odyssey runs two go-to-market models, and choosing between them is one of the earliest, highest-leverage decisions we make for any venture. Get it right and the product has a clear, cheap path to revenue. Get it wrong and you spend months fighting the physics of your own funnel.

The two models, briefly

Web-to-Web (W2W) keeps the entire experience on the web. A user finds you through search or an ad, lands on a web product, and pays — subscription or freemium — without ever leaving the browser. No app store, no install friction, instant iteration.

Web-to-App (W2A) uses the web as a conversion layer for a native app. A tuned web funnel captures demand, warms the user up, and hands them off to a mobile app built for retention and lifetime value. The web does the selling; the app does the keeping.

The model isn't a technology choice. It's a bet about where the value — and the friction — actually lives.

When we choose W2W

  • The job is done in one sitting. Tools, generators, document workflows, and anything a user "comes to do and leaves" thrive on the web, where there's nothing to install.
  • Search is the channel. If people describe their problem in a search box, a web product can meet them at the exact moment of intent and convert without a download.
  • Speed of iteration matters. We can ship a change to a web product in minutes. No review queues, no version fragmentation — just faster learning.

When we choose W2A

  • Value builds over time. Habit products — health, learning, wellbeing — earn their keep through daily use, notifications, and a home-screen presence only a native app provides.
  • Engagement needs the device. When the experience leans on camera, sensors, offline use, or push, the app isn't a nicety — it's the product.
  • The web can still sell better than the store. App-store pages are blunt instruments. A dedicated web funnel can qualify, educate, and price far more precisely, then send a warmer user to install.

How we actually decide

We don't argue about it in the abstract. For every venture we ask three questions, in order:

  • Where does the value accrue — in the session or over the weeks? Session value points to W2W; compounding value points to W2A.
  • Where does the user's intent live — in a search box or in a routine? Intent-at-a-moment favours web; intent-as-a-habit favours app.
  • What does the friction cost? An install is a tax. We only pay it when the native experience returns far more than the users we lose at the download step.

Often the honest answer is "start on the web." W2W lets us validate demand and economics with the least friction, and if a habit clearly emerges, we layer a W2A funnel and native app on top of a market we've already proven. The mistake we work hardest to avoid is choosing the heavier model out of ambition rather than evidence — building an app for a problem the browser could have solved, and paying the install tax for nothing.
