Where we play

What makes a market worth entering

We don't chase trends. Before we commit a team, a market has to clear four tests — and most don't.

Proven demand

People are already searching, paying, or hacking together workarounds. We look for a problem the market has confessed to — not one we have to convince it exists.

Winnable competition

Incumbents exist but are slow, expensive, or badly designed. Competition proves the market; a beatable field is where a small, fast team can take share.

Clear monetization

A believable path to revenue from day one — subscription, freemium, or in-app — with unit economics that work before we spend a euro scaling them.

Real retention

The problem recurs, so the product earns a place in someone's week. Retention is the only growth that compounds — we won't build something people use once.

From signal to scale

The lifecycle of a venture

Every product moves through the same gates — and has to earn its way to the next one.

01

Signal

A demand signal in one of our verticals turns into a written thesis: who hurts, how badly, and why now.

02

Validate

Landing pages, funnels, and small paid tests measure whether the market actually leans in before we build.

03

Build

A senior team ships the core loop in weeks on shared studio infrastructure, then iterates on live user behaviour.

04

Scale

Products that hit their retention and economics targets get full resources; the rest are pivoted or retired.
