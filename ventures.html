How we monetize

Two models, one playbook

Every venture ships in one of two formats — chosen by what converts best for the audience, never by what flatters our ambition.

Web-to-Web

Fully web-native products with subscription or freemium monetization. Fast to iterate, instantly accessible, and optimized for search and paid channels — no app store gatekeeping, and changes that ship in minutes.

It's the fastest way to validate demand and prove economics, which is why most ventures start here.

SubscriptionFreemiumSEO & paidInstant iteration

Web-to-App

Optimized web funnels that convert audiences directly into mobile app installs. We capture demand on the web, then hand off to a native experience built for retention and lifetime value.

We reach for it when value compounds over time — habit products where a home-screen presence is the point.

Where we play

What makes a market worth entering

Before we commit a team, a market has to clear four tests — and most don't.

01

Proven demand

People are already searching, paying, or hacking together workarounds. We look for a problem the market has confessed to — not one we have to convince it exists.

02

Winnable competition

Incumbents exist but are slow, expensive, or badly designed. Competition proves the market; a beatable field is where a small, fast team can take share.

03

Clear monetization

A believable path to revenue from day one — subscription, freemium, or in-app — with unit economics that work before we spend a euro scaling them.

04

Real retention

The problem recurs, so the product earns a place in someone's week. Retention is the only growth that compounds — we won't build something people use once.

From signal to scale

The lifecycle of a venture

Every product moves through the same gates — and has to earn its way to the next one.

1

Signal

A demand signal in one of our verticals turns into a written thesis: who hurts, how badly, and why now. If we can't state it in a sentence, we keep digging.

2

Validate

Landing pages, funnels, and small paid tests measure whether the market actually leans in — before a line of production code exists.

3

Build

A senior team ships the core loop in weeks on shared studio infrastructure, then iterates on live user behaviour rather than opinion.

4

Scale

Products that hit their retention and economics targets get full resources; the rest are pivoted or retired without drama.
